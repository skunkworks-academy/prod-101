# Marketing and Course Lifecycle Plan

## Positioning

**Course:** Mastering Productivity Hacks: Boost Your Efficiency and Achieve More in Less Time

**Primary audiences:**

- students improving study habits and time management;
- professionals improving focus, prioritisation and work execution;
- entrepreneurs and business owners managing competing priorities.

**Core promise:** practical productivity systems that learners can apply immediately without adding unnecessary complexity.

## Pre-launch

### Foundation

- Validate target learner pain points and search language.
- Finalise the course promise, outcomes and differentiators.
- Create the Udemy landing-page copy and instructor positioning.
- Build the course image, promo video and reusable visual system.
- Prepare launch coupons, tracking links and campaign attribution.

### Content campaign

Produce:

- eight module teaser posts;
- three short video clips;
- one productivity self-assessment;
- one lead magnet derived from the workbook;
- one LinkedIn article or newsletter edition;
- email launch sequence;
- partner/influencer outreach pack;
- course FAQ and objection-handling copy.

## Launch window

### Week 1

- Announce the course through LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Facebook and email.
- Publish instructor story and course rationale.
- Release limited launch coupon with a defined expiry.
- Invite verified learners to provide honest feedback after meaningful course use.
- Track source, clicks, enrolments, refunds and net payout.

### Week 2

- Publish practical clips and learner-use examples.
- Address common questions in a public FAQ post.
- Activate partner, affiliate and influencer outreach.
- Review landing-page conversion and revise weak copy or creative.

## Lifecycle management

### 30-day review

- Landing-page conversion.
- Enrolments and realised payout by channel.
- Refund rate.
- Average rating and review themes.
- Lecture drop-off and completion signals.
- Learner questions and support demand.
- High-priority defects and quick wins.

### 60-day review

- Campaign channel efficiency.
- Coupon and affiliate performance.
- Review velocity and rating stability.
- Content sections with weak engagement.
- Opportunities for new examples, templates or lectures.

### 90-day review

- Actual ROI and break-even status.
- Support cost and maintenance burden.
- Cross-sell or consulting leads generated.
- Decision: continue, optimise, re-record, reprice, bundle or retire.

### 180-day review

- Factual and platform currency.
- Broken links and obsolete screenshots.
- Learner feedback trends.
- Competitor and market-position changes.
- Potential localisation, cohort delivery or enterprise licensing.

## Marketing KPIs

| Funnel stage | Measures |
|---|---|
| Reach | impressions, video views, email delivery |
| Interest | clicks, landing-page visits, lead-magnet conversions |
| Purchase | enrolments, conversion rate, coupon performance, net payout |
| Experience | start rate, completion, rating, reviews, refunds |
| Retention | repeat engagement, updates viewed, community participation |
| Business value | ROI, time-to-break-even, leads, bundles and enterprise opportunities |

## Governance

Every campaign asset must have an owner, channel, audience, tracking link, publish date, expiry/review date and evidence link. Marketing claims must match the course content and must not promise guaranteed outcomes.
