# Project Charter

## Purpose

Convert the existing course outline, manuscript and introductory slide deck into a commercially publishable Udemy course and establish a repeatable lifecycle for content updates, quality assurance, marketing and financial performance review.

## Success criteria

The project is successful when:

- all eight modules have approved scripts, visuals, activities and knowledge checks;
- the final course contains 2–4 hours of edited video with clear audio and consistent branding;
- learner resources include a workbook, templates and action tools;
- content, instructional design, language, accessibility and platform compliance checks are complete;
- the course is published on Udemy by 4 September 2026;
- launch assets and campaigns are active by 18 September 2026;
- actual labour and cash investment are captured;
- break-even and ROI are reviewed no later than 4 December 2026;
- lifecycle reviews are scheduled at 30, 60, 90 and 180 days.

## Scope

### In scope

Course architecture, learning outcomes, module scripts, slides, workbook, activities, assessments, instructor recordings, editing, captions, thumbnails, promo video, Udemy listing, QA, proofing, launch marketing, learner feedback, updates, time tracking and ROI measurement.

### Out of scope

A separate LMS implementation, paid advertising spend above the approved budget, translation/localisation, accredited certification and live cohort delivery are excluded unless approved through a change issue.

## Roles

| Role | Accountability |
|---|---|
| Course owner | Scope, commercial decisions, final acceptance |
| Instructional designer | Learning design, outcomes, activities and assessment alignment |
| Content author | Scripts, workbook and references |
| Visual designer | Slide system, diagrams, workbook and marketing creative |
| Presenter | Recording readiness and delivery |
| Video editor | Edit, audio, captions, exports and media QA |
| QA reviewer | Content, language, accessibility, technical and release gates |
| Marketing owner | Listing, launch, campaign execution and lifecycle optimisation |

A person may hold multiple roles, but final QA must be performed independently from the original author where practical.

## Delivery controls

- **System of record:** GitHub Issues, milestones, pull requests and repository evidence.
- **Status values:** Backlog, Ready, In progress, In review, Blocked, Done.
- **Priority:** P0 critical, P1 high, P2 normal, P3 low.
- **Evidence:** Every completed task links to the relevant file, recording, review or platform page.
- **Time:** Planned and actual hours are recorded per issue.
- **Change control:** Scope changes require an issue stating schedule, effort and ROI impact.
- **Decision control:** Meeting decisions and assumptions are recorded as issues.

## Baseline assumptions

- Eight modules and 2–4 total learning hours.
- USD 99 is the indicative list price, not the assumed realised revenue per learner.
- Initial production effort baseline is 110 hours.
- Labour cost and realised net revenue are scenario inputs and must be replaced with actuals.

## Governance cadence

- Weekly 30-minute production review until publication.
- Daily blocker review while recording and editing are active.
- Release readiness review before Udemy submission.
- Commercial review at 30, 60 and 90 days after publication.
- Lifecycle review every six months or earlier when learner feedback or platform changes require it.
